What's actually behind the work

Every bioinformatics shop runs analyses. Almost none run them on this.

The differentiator isn't "we do analysis." Everyone does. It's the curated, mechanistic knowledge the analysis stands on — manually built and maintained for nearly four decades.

TRANSFAC®
Transcription factors and experimentally verified binding sites — the most comprehensive library of DNA motifs in existence.
TRANSPATH®
Causal, directional signal transduction reactions — not correlation networks. Curated reactions with direction, cellular context, and a primary-literature source.
HumanPSD™
Diseases, clinical trials, biomarkers, and drug targets with mechanistic annotations — connecting molecular biology to clinical context.

Mechanism, not correlation.

A general CRO runs the same omics pipelines everyone else does and hands you what changed. The harder question — why it changed, and what to do next — is where most engagements stop and ours begins.

Because the answer traces through regulation → pathway → disease, every conclusion is anchored to a record you can audit, not a black box you have to trust.

And the stakes are rarely the cost of the data itself. In biomedical R&D, the most expensive mistake is moving forward with the wrong biological hypothesis. This is the intelligence layer that lowers the budget you put at risk — before the expensive experiments begin.

AI + human accountability

A model can generate a conclusion. It can't take responsibility for one.

In a regulated pipeline, "the model said so" is not an answer. Someone has to stand behind the science — read the evidence, defend the call, and be reachable when it matters.

"The same DNA sequence is controlled by different transcription factors in different cell types. A model that pattern-matches will miss that. A person who knows the biology won't."
Prof. Dr. Alexander Kel · CEO & CSO, co-author of TRANSFAC®

A done-for-you engagement is built around a human expert who scopes the question, runs the mechanistic analysis, and is accountable for what comes back. That accountability is a property of the service model — a named scientist behind the result, not an automated report with no author.
